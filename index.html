What is Yasuni-ITT initiative?
The President of Ecuador, Rafael Correa at The General Assembly of the United Nations in 2007 announced his country's commitment to indefinitely maintain it's unexploited reserves of 846 million barrels of oil in the ITT (Ishpingo-Tambococha-Tiputini). This is equivalent to 20% of the country's reserves, located in the Yasuni National Park inside Ecuadorian Amazon territory. In effect, President Correa proposed that the international community financially contribute with at least 3.6 billion USD. This is a sum that represents half of the revenue lost to Ecuador for not\ exploiting these oil reserves.





How does the initiative work?
This money would go to a fund administered by the United Nations Development Program(UNDP) and Ecuador. Ecuador has signed an agreement with UNDP international trust to run the initiative. This initiative will avoid emission of approximatly 407 million tons of CO2, the main cause of global warming. This reduction is greater than the annual emissions of countries such as Brazil or France. The Yasuni National Park is the most important reservoir of biodiversity on the planet and home of two indigenous peoples in voluntary isolation in Ecuador, the Tagaeri and Taromenane. The explotaition of oil on this area will adversely affect the biodiversity and the indigenous populations.

In summary
Ecuador is commited to maintain unexploited, the oil reserves in the ITT Yasuni National Park indefinitely. Funds generated trought this initiative will be utilized to develop renewable energy sources in Ecuador. This will help maintain current ecosystems and protected areas, reforestation of damaged areas, promote social development, sustainable employment and improve energy efficiency.
President Correa said, '' This would be an extraordinary example of global collective action, that would allow not only reduce global warming, which benefits the whole planet, but also introduce a new economic logic for the 21st century, which assigns a value to things other than merchandise.''
